## Key Insights

### Content Trends
- **Medical professionals** (dermatologists, plastic surgeons) dominating YouTube with educational content
- **Patient-perspective influencers** on TikTok sharing raw experiences, costs, downtime
- **Korean medical tourism** is a significant niche with dedicated Japanese influencers
- **Before/after documentation** is highly valued by audiences

### Platform Preferences
- **YouTube**: Long-form educational content, detailed treatment explanations
- **Instagram**: Visual before/after, aesthetic clinic marketing, doctor personal brands
- **TikTok**: Short-form patient experiences, honest cost/pain discussions, younger demographic
- **X/Twitter**: Updates and quick tips from medical professionals

### Authenticity Concerns
- Clinics offer free/discounted procedures to influencers
- Water no Mori Clinic warns patients about influencer marketing practices
- Viewers seek transparency about sponsorship/compensation
- Medical advertising guidelines restrict certain claims

### Market Dynamics
- 10,000+ followers is typical threshold for clinic partnerships
- Follower engagement (likes, comments) affects compensation levels
- Multi-platform presence increases influencer value
- Patient testimonials valued over purely promotional content
