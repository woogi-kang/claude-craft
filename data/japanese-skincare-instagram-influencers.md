## Platform Trends & Insights

### Instagram vs. Other Platforms
- Many Japanese beauty influencers are multi-platform (Instagram + YouTube + TikTok)
- Instagram remains primary for visual before/after content
- Dermatologists particularly favor Instagram for educational content

### Content Types Popular in Japan
1. **Before/After Transformations**: Highly engaging for aesthetic procedures
2. **Product Reviews**: Honest, detailed reviews perform well
3. **Routine Breakdowns**: Step-by-step skincare routines
4. **Instagram Live**: Real-time Q&A sessions with followers
5. **Reels**: Short-form educational content and tips

### Follower Count Ranges
- **Medical Professionals**: 10K-216K followers
- **Beauty Experts**: 200K-1M followers
- **Celebrity/Model Influencers**: 500K-1M followers
- **Niche/Specialized**: 16K-100K followers

### Japanese Beauty Keywords (スキンケア用語)
- スキンケア (skincare)
- 美容皮膚科 (aesthetic dermatology)
- 皮膚科施術 (dermatological procedures)
- 美肌 (beautiful skin)
- エイジングケア (aging care)
- 毛穴ケア (pore care)
- 保湿 (moisturizing)
- 美白 (skin brightening)

## Summary

The Japanese skincare and dermatology influencer landscape on Instagram is dominated by three main categories:

1. **Medical Professionals (Dermatologists)**: Provide credibility and education, typically 10K-216K followers
2. **Beauty Experts**: Focus on techniques and methodology, typically 200K-1M followers
3. **Celebrity/Model Influencers**: Lifestyle and aspirational content, typically 500K-1M followers

**Top 3 by Follower Count**:
1. Kanzaki Megumi - 1,000,000
2. MAGGY - 983,000
3. Marilyn - 536,000

**Top Medical Professional**:
Dr. Kida - 216,000 followers (highest among dermatologists)

**Key Trends**:
- Multi-platform presence is essential
- Authenticity and honest reviews are highly valued
- Medical credentials add significant authority
- Visual before/after content performs best
- Educational content (science-backed) is growing in popularity
