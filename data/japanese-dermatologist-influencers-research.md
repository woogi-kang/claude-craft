## Key Trends & Observations

### Platform Preferences:
1. **Instagram:** Most popular platform for dermatologist influencers (visual nature suits before/after content)
2. **YouTube:** Growing platform for educational long-form content
3. **TikTok:** Emerging platform for younger audience engagement
4. **Twitter/X:** Supplementary platform for quick updates and engagement

### Content Types:
- Educational content on skin conditions and treatments
- Before/after treatment showcases
- Skincare product reviews and recommendations
- Lifestyle and wellness tips
- Clinic tours and behind-the-scenes content
- Collaborations with other physician influencers

### Common Characteristics:
- Board-certified dermatologists (皮膚科専門医)
- Multi-clinic operators or directors
- Published authors
- TV appearance experience
- Focus on evidence-based medicine
- Member of professional organizations

### Influencer D Corporation:
A physician influencer collective founded in 2022, featuring:
- 花房火月 (Hanafusa Hizuki)
- 玉城有紀 (Tamaki Yuki)
- 長尾沙也加 (Nagao Sayaka)
- 近藤千種 (Kondo Chigusa)

### Follower Count Ranges:
- **Mega Influencers (100K+):** Tomori Arata, Hanafusa Hizuki, Kobayashi Tomoko, Uehara Eri
- **Macro Influencers (50K-100K):** Multiple physicians
- **Mid-tier Influencers (10K-50K):** Majority of dermatologist influencers
- **Micro Influencers (<10K):** Many clinic-focused accounts
