## 1. Malaysian Medical Tourism to Korea: Statistics

Korea attracted a record **1,170,467 foreign patients** in 2024, a **93.2% increase** from 610,000 in 2023. The cumulative total since tracking began in 2009 reached 5.05 million patients. Korea surpassed its original 2027 target of 700,000 patients three years ahead of schedule.

**By region (2024):**
- East Asia: 810,000 (69.3%)

- **Southeast Asia: 112,000 (9.6%)**

### 1.2 Malaysia-Specific Estimates

Malaysia-specific patient numbers are not individually reported in Korea's official data. However, based on available data:

- Southeast Asia sent **112,000 patients** to Korea in 2024
- The ASEAN bloc includes Thailand (ranked in top 5), Singapore (ranked in top 5 by spending), Indonesia, Vietnam, Malaysia, Philippines, and others
- **Conservative estimate for Malaysia: 8,000-15,000 patients** (based on proportional share of ASEAN visitors, adjusting for Malaysia's smaller population vs. Thailand but higher income vs. Vietnam/Indonesia)
- Malaysia is mentioned alongside Singapore, Thailand, and India as "leading nations" in medical tourism

### 1.3 Korean Tourism Arrivals from Malaysia (General)

While specific medical tourism numbers for Malaysia are limited, general Korean tourism data shows:

- Korea welcomed **16.37 million total foreign tourists** in 2024
- ASEAN is a significant and growing source market
- Direct flights from KL to Seoul operate **78 flights per week** (6+ per day), indicating strong bilateral travel demand

## 2. Dermatology: The Dominant Draw

### 2.1 Dermatology Leads All Specialties

| Specialty | Patients (2024) | Share | Revenue |
|-----------|----------------|-------|---------|
| Dermatology | 705,000 | 56.6% | 585.5B KRW |

| Health Checkups | ~53,000 | 4.5% | — |

- Dermatology patients increased **194.9%** compared to 2023
- Since 2009, foreign dermatology patients have surged **117-fold**
- 82% of all foreign patients visited **clinics** (not hospitals)
- 85.4% of all foreign patients visited clinics in **Seoul**

### 2.2 Trending Treatments in Korea (2024-2026)

**Top 5 Trending Dermatology Procedures:**

1. **Pico Laser / Pico Toning** — Ultra-short pulse technology for pigmentation, melasma, freckles, uneven tone. 1,000x faster than traditional laser toning. Ideal for tropical-climate skin concerns. Minimal downtime.

2. **Rejuran Healer** — Salmon DNA-based polynucleotide skin booster. Improves elasticity, texture, and dermal repair. Requires 3-4 sessions. Strong demand from Southeast Asian patients.

3. **Exosome Therapy** — Cell communication enhancement for accelerated healing. Often combined with Pico laser or microneedling for synergy.

4. **Thermage FLX & Ultherapy** — RF and ultrasound energy for collagen regeneration and skin tightening.

5. **Stem Cell / Skin Booster Therapies** — Including Juvelook, Profhilo, and combination regenerative treatments.

**Combination Therapies (Korean Specialty):**
- Pico Laser + Exosome Therapy
- Thermage FLX + Rejuran Healer
- Microneedling + PRP + Skin Boosters
- Multi-layer approach treating different skin depths in single sessions

### 2.3 Treatments Relevant to Malaysian/Tropical Skin Concerns

Malaysian patients from tropical climates commonly seek treatment for:
- **Pigmentation / Melasma** — Sun exposure damage, hormonal pigmentation
- **Acne Scarring** — Humidity-related acne, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
- **Sun Damage** — UV exposure-related aging, dark spots
- **Skin Brightening** — "Glass skin" treatments, overall tone improvement
- **Pore Refinement** — Heat/humidity-related enlarged pores

- **Extended trip (1-2 weeks):** Full treatment course with follow-ups. Suitable for patients requiring multiple laser sessions or post-procedure monitoring.

**Sample One-Week Beauty Tourism Itinerary (from clinic data):**
- Day 1: Consultation + skin analysis

- Day 3: Recovery + sightseeing
- Day 4: Secondary treatment (e.g., Rejuran)
- Day 5: Recovery + K-beauty shopping

## 7. Competitive Landscape: Malaysia's Domestic Aesthetic Market

### 7.1 Malaysia as Medical Tourism Destination

Malaysia itself is a significant medical tourism destination:
- **1.52 million international patients** in 2024
- Revenue: ~$500M-$1.92B (depending on methodology)
- Target: $2.7B by 2030
- Growth rate: 14.6% CAGR to 2034

However, Malaysia's medical tourism is focused on:
- General healthcare (dental, orthopedic, cardiac)
- Health checkups
- Traditional/complementary medicine
- **Not a primary destination for advanced dermatology** compared to Korea

### 7.2 Malaysian Aesthetic Clinic Market

- **79+ aesthetic clinics** listed in major Malaysian review platforms
- Kuala Lumpur is the center of Malaysia's aesthetic industry
- Clinics are well-equipped but lack Korea's specialization depth
- Malaysian clinics compete on convenience and language familiarity
- Local clinics offer Rejuran, Botox, fillers, and lasers but with less variety

### 7.3 Why Malaysians Still Travel to Korea

1. **Technology superiority**: Korean clinics pioneer treatments (Pico, Exosome, advanced combination therapies)
2. **Higher volume = more expertise**: 571 dermatology clinics in Seoul alone; doctors perform thousands of procedures annually
3. **Price competitiveness**: Comparable or cheaper than Malaysian premium clinics for advanced treatments
4. **Cultural cachet**: "Getting it done in Korea" carries social prestige
5. **Shopping integration**: K-beauty shopping trip combined with treatments
6. **Broader treatment menu**: Treatments not yet available or rare in Malaysia
