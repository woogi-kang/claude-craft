## Price Factors

### Why Korea is Cheaper

1. **Competition density**: 1,500+ dermatology clinics in Seoul alone
2. **Scale economics**: High patient volume enables lower per-unit costs
3. **National health insurance**: Base infrastructure reduces overhead
4. **Equipment leasing**: Aggressive leasing competition for devices
5. **Marketing competition**: Clinics compete on price transparency

### What's Included in Korean Prices

- Typically includes: consultation, anesthesia cream, procedure, basic aftercare
- NOT typically included: initial consultation fee (some clinics), medication, follow-up visits
- Tax: Korean prices usually include VAT; may offer tax-free for foreigners

### What's Included in Japanese Prices

- Typically includes: consultation, procedure only
- Extra charges common: anesthesia (2,000-5,000 JPY), aftercare products, re-examination fee
- Tax: displayed price may be pre-tax (add 10% consumption tax)

## Travel Cost Consideration

When comparing total cost, factor in:
- Flight: 30,000-60,000 JPY round trip (Japan-Korea)
- Accommodation: 5,000-15,000 JPY per night
- Break-even point: treatments totaling 5-10万円 Korean savings make the trip worthwhile
- Strategy: bundle multiple treatments in one trip for maximum savings
